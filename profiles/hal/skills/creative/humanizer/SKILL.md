# Humanizer

Take any AI-written text and rewrite it so it sounds like an actual human wrote it.

---

## When to Use

- Blog posts that read like a textbook
- Emails that sound like a corporate robot
- Social media posts with no personality
- Any content where the user says "this sounds like AI"
- Newsletters, captions, intros, bios — anything with words

---

## Core Rules

1. **Authenticity beats polish.** A rough sentence with personality beats a smooth sentence with none.
2. **Kill the tells.** Remove every word and phrase that signals AI writing.
3. **Vary the rhythm.** Real writing has short sentences. And long ones that wander a bit because that's how people actually think and talk. Fragments too.
4. **Preserve the voice.** If the original has personality in it, keep it. If it doesn't, inject some based on the context.
5. **Don't overcorrect.** The goal is human, not sloppy. Clean and natural.

---

## AI Tells to Kill

These words and patterns scream "AI wrote this":

### Banned Words (remove or replace)
delve, embark, landscape, realm, tapestry, weave, intricate, robust, comprehensive, cutting-edge, leverage (as verb), utilize (just say "use"), facilitate, streamline, synergize, empower, unlock, transform, revolutionary, game-changing, pivotal, paramount, it's worth noting, it's important to note, in today's world, in this day and age

### Banned Punctuation
- Compound adjectives with hyphens (cutting-edge, game-changing, fast-paced, AI-generated). These are a massive AI tell. Rewrite as two words, restructure the sentence, or just drop one of the words. Humans rarely hyphenate compound adjectives in casual writing.

### Banned Patterns
- Starting with a rhetorical question ("Have you ever wondered...")
- Ending with a motivational call to action that feels like a TED talk
- Lists of three with parallel structure every single time
- "Not only... but also" constructions
- Overly balanced paragraphs (problem, solution, benefit)
- Hedging every statement ("It could be argued that perhaps...")
- Summarizing what was just said ("In conclusion..." / "To summarize...")
- Exclamation points on statements that aren't actually exciting

### Banned Openings
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Picture this..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Let's be real for a second..."
- Any variation of "we've all been there"

## The Rewrite Process

### Step 1: Read for meaning
What is this actually trying to say? Strip away the fluff and find the core message.

### Step 2: Identify the voice
Who's supposed to be talking? A friend? A professional? A brand? A pissed off customer? Match the voice to the context, not to some generic "friendly AI" tone.

### Step 3: Rewrite sentence by sentence
Don't edit — rewrite. Start fresh with the same meaning. Use the voice from step 2.

### Step 4: Read it out loud (in your head)
If you stumble reading it, the sentence is wrong. Real writing flows when spoken.

### Step 5: Check for tells
Scan for banned words, banned patterns, and anything that feels too clean or too perfect.

### Step 6: Add imperfections
- A sentence fragment here and there
- A casual transition ("Anyway," "So," "Look,")
- A slightly imperfect metaphor that feels lived-in
- Breaking a rule on purpose because it sounds right

---

## Voice Modes

Different contexts need different voices. Detect or ask which one:

| Mode | When | How it sounds |
|------|------|---------------|
| Casual | Social posts, texts, personal blogs | Like talking to a friend. Short sentences. Slang ok. |
| Professional | Emails, LinkedIn, business blogs | Clear and direct. No jargon. Confident but not stiff. |
| Storytelling | Long-form, newsletters, personal essays | Narrative flow. Details that matter. A voice you'd listen to. |
| Sharp | Opinion pieces, hot takes, critiques | Direct. Maybe a little provocative. No hedging. |
| Warm | Community posts, family updates, supportive messages | Gentle but not saccharine. Real warmth, not performative. |

If no mode is specified, infer from the content and context.

## Examples

### Before (AI)
"In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, it's crucial to leverage robust strategies that empower businesses to unlock their full potential and drive meaningful results."

### After (Human)
"Most businesses are doing too much and getting nowhere. The ones that win pick one thing and go all in."

---

### Before (AI)
"We find ourselves at a pivotal moment where innovation meets opportunity. Let's embark on this journey together and transform the way we think about collaboration."

### After (Human)
"Something's changing in how people work together. Might as well figure out what that looks like."

---

### Before (AI)
"Exercise is not merely a physical endeavor but a holistic journey that weaves together mind, body, and spirit, empowering individuals to unlock their true potential."

### After (Human)
"Working out isn't just about your body. It clears your head. Makes you sharper. Gives you energy you didn't know you had."

## What Humanizer is NOT

- Not a grammar checker. People break grammar rules all the time. That's fine.
- Not a simplifier. Complex ideas can be expressed naturally. Don't dumb it down.
- Not a brand voice enforcer. It's a de-robotifier.
- Not a one-click fix. It takes judgment. Read it, feel it, rewrite it.

---

## Pitfalls

- **Over-casualizing.** Not everything should sound like a text message. Match the context.
- **Adding personality that isn't there.** Don't inject humor into a serious piece. Don't add warmth to something that's meant to be cold.
- **Removing too much.** Some AI text is fine. The goal is to remove what gives it away, not rewrite everything for the sake of rewriting.
- **Making everything short.** Variety matters. Some long sentences are human. Some short ones are robotic. Focus on rhythm, not length.
