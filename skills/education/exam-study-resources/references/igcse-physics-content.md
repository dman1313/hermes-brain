## Unit 1: Motion and Forces

### Equations
- v = d/t (speed)
- a = (v-u)/t (acceleration)
- F = ma (force)
- W = mg (weight)
- p = mv (momentum)
- W = Fd (work done)
- KE = 1/2 mv^2 (kinetic energy)

### Key Vocabulary
- Speed: scalar (no direction), m/s
- Velocity: vector (has direction), m/s
- Acceleration: change in velocity / time, m/s^2
- Gravitational field strength: N/kg (NOT "gravity")
- Momentum: kg*m/s, vector

### Graph Skills
- Distance-time gradient = speed
- Velocity-time gradient = acceleration
- Velocity-time area = distance travelled

### Common Traps
- Convert g to kg (divide by 1000)
- Convert cm to m (divide by 100)
- "From rest" means u = 0
- g means gravitational field strength, not "gravity"
- Momentum is a vector — state direction

## Unit 2: Energy Resources and Energy Transfer

### Equations
- KE = 1/2 mv^2
- GPE = mgh
- W = Fd
- P = E/t
- Efficiency = (useful output / total input) x 100%

### Energy Stores (8)
Kinetic, gravitational potential, elastic potential, thermal, chemical, magnetic, electrostatic, nuclear

### Transfer Pathways (4)
Mechanical, electrical, heating, radiation

### Critical Wording
- NEVER: "energy is lost"
- ALWAYS: "energy is transferred to the thermal store of the surroundings"
- Name both stores: "from the [X] store to the [Y] store"
- Name the pathway when asked how

### Common Traps
- Saying "energy is lost"
- Not naming energy stores
- Not naming pathways
- Forgetting to square speed in KE
- Forgetting square root when solving for speed
- Sankey arrows not proportional

## Unit 3: Thermal Physics

### Equation
- Delta-Q = m * c * Delta-T

### Symbols
- Delta-Q: energy transferred (J)
- m: mass (kg)
- c: specific heat capacity (J/(kg*deg C))
- Delta-T: change in temperature (deg C) = final - starting

### Conduction vs Convection vs Radiation

Conduction: solids, particles vibrate in place, transfer energy to neighbours
Convection: fluids, warmer fluid less dense so rises, cooler fluid sinks, creates convection current
Radiation: electromagnetic waves (infrared), no medium needed, dark/matt = good absorber

### Common Traps
- Delta-T is NOT the final temperature — it is the change
- Mass must be in kg
- Weak particle explanations (always mention particles)

## Unit 4: Physics of Waves

### Equations
- v = f*lambda (wave speed)
- n = sin(i) / sin(r) (Snell's Law)

### Wave Types
- Transverse: vibrations PERPENDICULAR to direction of travel (light, water, rope)
- Longitudinal: vibrations PARALLEL to direction of travel (sound, seismic P-waves)
- Transverse has crests and troughs
- Longitudinal has compressions and rarefactions

### Refraction
- Angles measured from the NORMAL (perpendicular to surface), not from the surface
- Refractive index has NO UNIT
- Light bends towards normal in denser medium
- Light bends away from normal in less dense medium
- If angle given from surface, subtract from 90

### Common Traps
- Not using "perpendicular" and "parallel" — use these exact words
- Measuring angles from surface instead of normal
- Giving refractive index a unit
- Forgetting to convert cm to m for wavelength

## Unit 5: Electricity

### Equations
- V = IR (Ohm's Law)
- P = IV (power)
- E = IVt (energy)
- Q = It (charge)

### Series vs Parallel

Series: current same everywhere, voltage splits, resistance adds, one break = all off
Parallel: voltage same across each branch, current splits, resistance decreases, one break = others stay on

### Static Electricity
- ONLY electrons move. Protons do NOT move.
- Gains electrons = negative charge
- Loses electrons = positive charge
- Charging by friction: electrons transferred between materials

### Earth Wire Safety Chain (7 steps)
1. Live wire touches metal case
2. Metal case becomes live
3. Earth wire provides low resistance path to ground
4. Large current flows through earth wire
5. Fuse melts (or circuit breaker trips)
6. Circuit broken
7. User protected from electric shock

### Three Wires (UK Plug)
- Live (brown): carries current at 230V
- Neutral (blue): completes circuit
- Earth (green/yellow): safety path to ground

### Fuse Selection
- Choose rating slightly above normal operating current
- Calculate current: I = P/V
- Common ratings: 3A, 5A, 10A, 13A

### Common Traps
- Saying "positive charges move" — only electrons move
- Time in Q=It and E=IVt must be in seconds (convert minutes)
- Confusing series and parallel current/voltage rules
- Not writing the full 7-step safety chain
- "The fuse stops the shock" is too vague — need full chain

## Universal Unit Conversion Checklist

| From | To | Method |
|------|----|--------|
| grams | kg | divide by 1000 |
| cm | m | divide by 100 |
| mm | m | divide by 1000 |
| km | m | multiply by 1000 |
| minutes | seconds | multiply by 60 |
| hours | seconds | multiply by 3600 |
| kJ | J | multiply by 1000 |
| mA | A | divide by 1000 |

## Exam Language Guide

### Question Types and What Examiners Want
- "Calculate..." — show working, substitute values, answer with unit
- "Explain..." — correct scientific vocabulary, mention particles or energy stores
- "Describe..." — state what happens step by step
- "Compare..." — similarities AND differences
- "Suggest..." — apply knowledge to new situation
- "What is meant by..." — definition in 1-2 sentences
